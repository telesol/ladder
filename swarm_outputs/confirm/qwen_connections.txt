### Analysis of Bitcoin Puzzle K-Values Discoveries

Let's delve into each discovery and explore the mathematical connections that tie them together.

#### Discovery 1: d[n] Minimization Rule
For \( n = 4 \) to \( 70 \), \( d[n] \) is chosen such that \( m[n] = \frac{2^n - adj[n]}{k[d[n]]} \) is minimized. This rule seems to stabilize the sequence by selecting \( k[d[n]] \) optimally for each step.

#### Discovery 2: Gap Puzzle Oscillation
The gap puzzle oscillation at 5-step intervals from \( n = 70 \) onwards shows a perfectly alternating pattern (DOWN-UP-DOWN-UP). This behavior is observed in the ratio \( c[n] = \frac{k[n]}{2^n} \).

#### Discovery 3: 10-Step Intervals
For 10-step jumps, the pattern is not simple and alternates irregularly. This suggests more complex dynamics at larger steps.

#### Discovery 4: adj[n] Sign Pattern
The sign pattern of \( adj[n] = k[n] - 2k[n-1] \) follows a ++- pattern for small \( n \), breaking at \( n = 17 \). This indicates a transition in the sequence behavior.

#### Discovery 5: Bootstrap Values
Bootstrap values \( k[1]=1, k[2]=3, k[3]=7 \) follow Mersenne numbers, transitioning to \( k[4] = 8 \), and \( m[4] \approx 7\pi \).

#### Discovery 6: Closed-Form x_n
The closed-form formula for \( x_n \) involves hyperbolic cosine functions. The sequence of \( m \)-values encodes the signs of the y-coordinates.
