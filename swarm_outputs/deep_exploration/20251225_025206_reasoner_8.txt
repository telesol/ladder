The sequence follows a specific pattern where each term is obtained by doubling the previous term and adding one. This results in terms that are one less than powers of two, known as Mersenne numbers. 

For example:
- Starting with 1,
- The next term is \(2 \times 1 + 1 = 3\),
- Then \(2 \times 3 + 1 = 7\),
- Followed by \(2 \times 7 + 1 = 15\), and so on.

Thus, the sequence is given by \(k[n] = 2^n - 1\).
